Etymology

There are two competing arguments on the origin of kkondae.

The first theory claims that the word kondaegi, which means a pupa in the South Gyeongsang Province dialect, is the origin: The folded skin of a pupa reminds the wrinkles of an old man, so the word might have become a representation of an old man.

The other theory suggests the French nobility title fr:comte (count) is the basis of the word kkondae.
During the Japanese occupation of Korea, pro-Japanese politicians such as Lee Wan-yong received nobility titles from the Japanese government.
They proudly called themselves kkondae, hence it might have been developed into a slang later.

The late 20th century

Originally, the word kkondae meant simply an old man in a profane way.
During the late 1960s when industrialization and westernization were in full swing in South Korea, the word also began to refer to (classroom) teachers.
Students, who were not satisfied with the outdated school environment, would dismiss teachers from the older generation as old-fashioned beings.
Hence, the word kkondae became a slang used by students.
